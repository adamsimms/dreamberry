Dreamberry

A machine present at a place it can no longer photograph. Real weather, a remembered frame, dreamed anew each hour.

When the Cloudberry camera failed on the island in 2018, the hourly view it kept went dark. Dreamberry picks the signal back up — not with a photograph, but with an inference. Every hour it reads the live weather at Pinchard's Island and dreams the window the camera used to hold, anchored to the real archive it left behind.

The weather is the only thing still true. The rocks are remembered and invented; the wind, cloud, fog, and sea are real, right now, at the island. Every frame is labeled generated.
